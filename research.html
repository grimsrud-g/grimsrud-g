Ongoing · UMN MIDB

SCAN & Methylphenidate

Despite a well-established molecular underpinning, a lot about how methylphenidate, the most commonly prescribed stimulant medication for ADHD, impacts the brain at a macro-scale is unknown. Our results suggest that the somato-cognitive action network plays a key role in how methylphenidate exerts positive effects on brain function. Worked conducted under the mentorship of Dr. Damien Fair.

Ongoing · UMN MIDB

PFM: Movie-Watching vs Standard Resting State

Precision Functional Mapping is a powerful tool to map individual differences in functional brain organization. But are these networks stable regardless of what you are watching when your resting-state data is collected? The answer to this question will come from ongoing work continued by Dr. Kate Godfrey at UMN. Work conducted under mentorship of Dr. Steve Nelson at the Masonic Institute for the Developing Brain.

Papers I Find Interesting

A curated, personal selection — not a comprehensive review, just things that made me think differently.

01
Parkinson's Disease as a Somato-Cognitive Action Network Disorder
Ren, Zhang, Dahmani, Gordon et al. · Nature · 2026
A landmark demonstration that precision functional mapping has real clinical teeth — targeting the SCAN specifically more than doubled treatment response in PD patients across DBS, TMS, and pharmacology. This is what translational neuroimaging should look like.
02
Linking Spontaneous Activity of Single Cortical Neurons and the Underlying Functional Architecture
Tsodyks, Kenet, Grinvald & Arieli · Science · 1999
A foundational piece for anyone who wants to link BOLD signal back to neural activity — showing that the firing rate of individual neurons is tightly coupled to large-scale population activity patterns. The bridge between the cellular and systems levels.
